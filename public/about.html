WHO ARE WE?

We are Bristol University's Opera Society!

Our society provides many fantastic opportunities to get involved in the unique world of Opera, whether you are a singer, musician, or just an opera fanatic! We put on a multitude of events each year and are always keen to encourage people to get involved in all kinds of different ways.

Each year, we stage our annual main show, performed to the public, in March/April time. Past years have seen a range of different productions: from Mozart's Don Giovanni to Janacek's The Cunning Little Vixen.

When we're not performing on stage, we hold a number of smaller events. These include regular arias concerts and recitals, which give members other chances to perform, alumni masterclasses, opera screening socials, trips to opera houses, termly formals and Performing Arts nights out.

We are always on the lookout for new members; instrumentalists, singers, conductors, directors, producters or anyone who fancies trying something new - we will welcome you with open arms!

PRESIDENT
WALTER HALL (HE/HIM)

president.bops@gmail.com

Walter is a Second Year student of Classical Studies. He joined BOpS last year and directed Die Fledermaus in March 2022, and also went on to co-direct a sketch for a DramSoc and Spotlights showcase in June. Walter is directing BOpS’ 2023 production of Donizetti’s The Elixir of Love.
He has been involved in music, theatre and opera from a young age; as a performer, his credits in opera include Noye’s Fludde, L’Etoile and two new commissions: Belongings and Agreed at Glyndebourne opera house, where he was also a Junior Artist from 2018-19. Walter was a panellist on the youth jury for Glyndebourne and Sky Arts’ Opera Cup competition in March 2020.
He is immensely grateful to the society for being elected president and is excited to be working with a fantastic committee to continue to provide Bristol students with the opportunity of discovering and exploring the world of opera. He is committed to making opera a more accessible art form and wants BOpS to be a society that welcomes people to try out opera who have never had the opportunity to before, as well as to provide those who have previously to continue developing their talents.
